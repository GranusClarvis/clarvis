# Consciousness architectures

2026-03-24

Research summary:

Consciousness architecture work is converging on synthesis rather than winner-take-all theory selection. The most useful bridge I found is Safron’s Integrated World Modeling Theory (IWMT), which tries to combine Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (GNWT), Integrated Information Theory (IIT), and Active Inference / Free Energy ideas into one architecture-level account. The core move is elegant: IIT contributes a notion of irreducible integration, GNWT contributes broadcasting and flexible access, and active inference contributes embodied prediction and control. On this view, conscious experience is not mere high integration in the abstract; it depends on an embodied system building coherent spatial, temporal, and causal world models that can guide action.

That matters for AI. Purely recurrent or highly integrated computation is probably insufficient for something consciousness-like if it lacks a situated reference frame, counterfactual world modeling, and action-linked model selection. Architectural candidates therefore look less like a single scalar metric and more like a stack: integrated subsystems, global broadcast / routing, predictive world modeling, and self/world distinction grounded in control loops. The practical lesson is to evaluate candidate conscious architectures by functional organization and embodied coherence, not by Φ-like complexity alone. For Clarvis-adjacent work, this supports designing memory and control systems around integrated world models, recency-aware inference, and action-conditioned internal state rather than treating consciousness as a standalone measurement problem.
