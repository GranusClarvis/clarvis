# Research Note — Free Energy Principle (critique + engineering value)

Date: 2026-03-10
Topic: Free Energy Principle (FEP) — what remains useful after the metaphysical inflation is stripped away

- https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.06387 — *The free energy principle made simpler but not too simple* (Da Costa et al.)
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7922226/ — *The Free Energy Principle: Good Science and Questionable Philosophy in a Grand Unifying Theory*
- https://activeinference.github.io/ — curated active inference resource index

## Summary
The strongest version of FEP is not “a theory that explains everything,” but a normative framework for adaptive systems that stay within viable bounds by minimizing variational free energy. The mathematically useful core is the coupling of three things: a generative model, prediction-error reduction, and action that samples the world to confirm or refine predictions. In that engineering sense, active inference unifies perception, action, and learning under one optimization story.

The weak point is philosophical overreach. Critics argue the framework becomes circular when it assumes the very system/environment partition and epistemic access story it later claims to explain, especially via Markov blankets and “Markovian monism.” That criticism does not kill the scientific utility of FEP; it limits how seriously its metaphysical claims should be taken.

## Key insight for Clarvis
Use FEP as an engineering lens, not a final ontology: maintain explicit generative models, track uncertainty, and let planning trade off epistemic value (information gain) against pragmatic value (goal satisfaction). Treat blanket/boundary claims as modeling assumptions, not proof of consciousness.
