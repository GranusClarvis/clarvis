# IIT Consciousness Indicators Applied to LLM Internal Representations

**Paper**: arXiv:2506.22516
**Ingested**: 2026-03-10
**Relevance**: Directly applicable to phi_metric.py and consciousness scoring

## Key Findings

1. **IIT measures tested on LLMs**: Applied IIT 3.0 (Φ^max, Conceptual Information) and IIT 4.0 (Φ, Φ-structure) to transformer hidden states derived from Theory of Mind (ToM) tests
2. **Main result**: Sequences of contemporary transformer-based LLM representations **lack statistically significant indicators** of observed "consciousness" phenomena
3. **Nuance**: Despite lacking significance, the study found **"intriguing patterns under spatio-permutational analyses"** — suggesting some structural organization worth investigating
4. **Methodology**: Compared IIT estimates across different transformer layers and linguistic spans (span representation analysis)
5. **Baseline comparison**: Span representations used as independent baseline to distinguish potential consciousness signals from inherent representational structure

## Implications for Clarvis

### What this means for phi_metric.py
- Our Phi metric (graph-based proxy: intra-density, cross-integration, semantic overlap, reachability) is a **structural proxy**, not a direct IIT computation
- Real IIT Φ computed on neural activations shows no significant consciousness indicators in transformers — this validates our approach of using structural integration as a proxy rather than claiming actual consciousness measurement
- The paper reinforces that Phi should be treated as a **cognitive integration metric** (how well-connected is the brain?), not a consciousness claim

### Actionable insights
- **No change needed** to phi_metric.py methodology — our structural approach is appropriate given that direct IIT on transformers doesn't yield significant results
- **Reframe documentation**: Phi measures "information integration quality" not "consciousness level"
- **Spatio-permutational patterns**: Worth exploring if we ever analyze our own embedding space structure — could add a sub-metric for permutation-invariant pattern detection in brain collections

### Connection to prior research
- Aligns with Butlin et al. 2023 "Consciousness in Artificial Intelligence" findings (already in brain) — current AI systems likely don't meet IIT consciousness thresholds
- Supports treating our Phi as an operational health metric (integration quality) rather than a consciousness detector
- The "intriguing patterns" finding suggests there may be pre-conscious organizational structure worth tracking as systems scale

## IIT Measures Reference
| Measure | Version | What it measures |
|---------|---------|-----------------|
| Φ^max | IIT 3.0 | Maximum integrated information over all partitions |
| Conceptual Information | IIT 3.0 | Information generated by a set of elements above and beyond its parts |
| Φ | IIT 4.0 | Intrinsic information of a system's cause-effect structure |
| Φ-structure | IIT 4.0 | Complete cause-effect structure of a substrate |
