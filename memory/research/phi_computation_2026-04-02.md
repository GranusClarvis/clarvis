# Phi computation — research summary (2026-04-02)

Integrated Information Theory treats consciousness-relevant structure as a system’s irreducible cause-effect power, summarized by Φ, but exact computation remains the central bottleneck. PyPhi, the reference implementation for IIT 3.0-style analyses, can unfold the full cause-effect structure of small discrete Markovian binary systems, yet the search over mechanisms, purviews, and partitions scales combinatorially; in practice, exact analysis is usually limited to very small systems. Subsequent work evaluating approximations and heuristic measures found that some proxies correlate strongly with exact Φ on small synthetic networks, but they do not remove the core tractability problem and should not be treated as interchangeable with ground-truth Φ.

IIT 4.0 sharpens the theory conceptually by reformulating intrinsic information and explicit causal relations, but that conceptual cleanup does not magically make computation cheap; it mainly clarifies what should be computed. The practical lesson is that there are really two distinct research tracks: (1) exact Φ/CES computation for toy systems, useful for theory validation and benchmark generation, and (2) scalable surrogate metrics for empirical work, useful for ranking, screening, or estimating consciousness-related structure in larger systems. For Clarvis, the relevant transfer is methodological: treat exact Φ as a gold-standard evaluator for small benchmark environments, then optimize retrieval or memory architectures against cheaper proxy measures rather than attempting exact Φ-style computation at production scale.
